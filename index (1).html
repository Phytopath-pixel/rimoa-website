Introduction

The Global Significance of Cocoa

Cocoa (Theobroma cacao L.) is one of the most economically and culturally significant tropical crops in the world — the primary raw material for the multi-billion-dollar chocolate industry. Countries like Côte d'Ivoire and Ghana account for more than 60% of global production.

Current Challenges

Key Cocoa Disease Threats

Cocoa productivity is severely constrained by fungal diseases. These pathogens lead to significant yield losses and affect sustainability of cocoa cultivation worldwide.

🦠
Black Pod Disease
Phytophthora spp.
Most destructive cocoa disease globally. Causes rapid pod rot and can devastate entire harvests within days under humid conditions.
🌿
Witches' Broom & Frosty Pod
Moniliophthora spp.
Causes abnormal broom-like growth and white frost coating on pods, severely reducing yield and quality in affected farms.
🍂
Anthracnose & Canker
Colletotrichum spp.
Causes dark lesions on leaves, pods and stems. Particularly prevalent in Southeast Asian cocoa farms including KMITL trial sites.
Research Objectives

Seven Research Goals

A comprehensive research program from pathogen identification to field-scale organic production comparison.

01
Isolate and identify morphological and molecular phylogeny of major cocoa pathogens (Phytophthora palmivora, P. megakarya, Fusarium spp., Colletotrichum spp.)
02
Isolate and identify Rhizobacteria from rhizosphere soil of cocoa and screen their properties for biological control.
03
Screen selected Rhizobacteria and other beneficial microbes to control cacao pathogens.
04
Characterize antibiotic biosynthesis, siderophore production, enzymatic hydrolysis, volatile compounds, and pesticide resistance.
05
Prepare formulations of Bio-stimulant and Bioremediation from selected beneficial microbes.
06
Pot Experiment — Quantification of Phytoalexin Production in Cocoa Seedlings under microbial treatment.
07
Develop an integrating method for organic cocoa production and compare with chemical methods in field trials.

Research Highlights
Key findings from ongoing studies

P. palmivora CPC1 Morphologically Confirmed

Isolate CPC1 from infected cocoa pod exhibited ovoid to ellipsoidal sporangia on Water Agar, with robust mycelial growth on PDA and V8 juice agar confirming morphological identity.

PathogenP. palmivoraChumphon

Pathogenicity Confirmed on Cocoa & Durian

Leaf detached method showed clear lesion development within 7 DPI on cocoa leaves. Cross-pathogenicity on durian confirmed at 4 DPI with full necrosis by 7 DPI.

PathogenPathogenicity Test7 DPI

Siderophore Production Confirmed in Cp07 & Cp03

Distinct orange/yellow halos on CAS agar confirmed positive siderophore secretion — a key biological control trait indicating iron-chelating ability to suppress pathogens.
